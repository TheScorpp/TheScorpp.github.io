About Me

My interest in physics began rather unexpectedly. During a PT class in school, one of my teachers took me to a higher class where she was teaching some science topic that I have long since forgotten as a punishment because I was talking too much. I do not remember what the lesson was about, but I remember being completely fascinated. Ever since that day, I have been hooked!

The second major source of inspiration came when I first read about dark matter and dark energy during my bachelor's. The idea that most of the Universe is made up of things we have no idea about blew my mind, and I have never looked back since. The thought that the cosmos still holds such profound mysteries is what drew me towards cosmology and continues to motivate my research today.

I am interested in dark energy and, more broadly, in the fundamental questions of physics. I see my current work in cosmology as a first step towards exploring deeper questions, such as the relationship between gravity and quantum physics and even the foundations of quantum mechanics itself.

What I enjoy most about research is the freedom to play with ideas: to ask "what if?", build models, and then (hopefully) rigorously test whether those ideas make any scientific sense. There is something deeply satisfying about taking an intuition and turning it into a coherent physical picture.

One of the things that fascinates me the most is that many of these questions have been worked on by some of the brightest minds in history, and yet we still do not have all the answers. Progress is often slow, and the mysteries remain. I find that incredibly exciting.

I would love to contribute, even in a very small way, to our understanding of these problems. Even if my contribution amounts to just 0.1%, I would consider myself fortunate to have been a part of the journey.

Apart from this, I like Novels, One Piece and animating! More on this, later!

Only time will tell whether these ambitions bear fruit or remain beautifully persistent delusions; either way, the pursuit itself is already worth it.
