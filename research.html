Current Research

The accelerated expansion of the Universe remains one of the greatest mysteries in modern cosmology. My current research focuses on Interacting Dark Energy (IDE), a class of models in which dark matter and dark energy exchange energy through a non-gravitational coupling.

I am interested in developing physically motivated IDE models, studying their dynamical behaviour, and testing their viability using cosmological observations such as the CMB, BAO, Type Ia supernovae, and large-scale structure data. More broadly, this work aims to understand whether new physics in the dark sector can provide a consistent explanation for late-time cosmic acceleration and the observed tensions in cosmological parameters.
